Mountaineering Institutes in India

Why Mountaineering

Physical benefits; because mountaineering combines hiking, walking and climbing on an uneven and steep landscape, mountaineers become fit along the course. The sport requires aerobic conditioning and endurance as preparation; this as well keeps the body in shape and adds physical strength to mountaineers.

Importance of Mountaineering in India

India lying in foothills of Mighty Himalayas is very rich in producing Great Mountaineers to the world and the mountaineering is also a great & important sports for India. Himalayan attracts many mountaineers from all over the global to plan expeditions and summit peaks all over Himalayas. Himalayas also gives opportunity to the institutions to teach the skill of mountaineering to young trainees to get qualified to conquer any 7k peak in the world.
Mountaineering is quite popular in India, since the entire northern and north-eastern borders are the Himalayas, the highest mountain range in the world. The apex body in India is the Indian Mountaineering Foundation, which is affiliated to the International Federation of Sport Climbing.

Some Premier Mountaineering Institutes in India

  • Swami Vivekanand Institutes of Mountaineering, Mount Abu
  • Himalayan Mountaineering Institute, Darjeeling
  • Nehru Institute of Mountaineering, Uttarkashi
  • Indian Institute of Skiing and Mountaineering, Gulmarg
  • Jawahar Institute of Mountaineering and Winter Sports, Pahalgam
  • National Institute of Mountaineering and Allied Sports (NIMAS), Dirang, Arunachal Pradesh
  • Atal Bihari Vajpayee Institute of Mountaineering and Allied Sports (ABVIMAS), Manali
